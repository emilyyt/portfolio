Gadgets & Sensors HCI Masters Course

Final Project: Tamagotchi

Project Overview

Back in college, I took a Gadgets & Sensors course, where we got a chance to play with Arduinos and sensors. Some of the projects I completed during the period of the course were:

  • A remake of Snake
  • My own Arduino
  • A project of my choosing

I chose to make my own tamagotchi for the final project and it is actually receiving some interest on the internet!

First, I planned out the life stages of the pet: egg, toddler, teen, adult. Next, I created bitmap images of each stages plus other UI elements on the screen such as some grass and the life meter. Technical wise, I have event handlers for both sensors. If the value of either sensors fall outside the optimal range, and the life meter goes under, the pet stage will downgrade and the pet will eventually die. I also played around with some animations to make the pet appear more alive by drawing the pet image every 500ms, alternating between one pixel above and below.

The light sensor and the thermostat both contribute to the well-being of the pet. I would like to think, the light sensor is equivalent to giving food and sunlight, while thermostat represents the shelter environment the pet is in.
